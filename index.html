Search Method

Systematic searches were carried out across five databases (PubMed, CINAHL Plus, PsycInfo, Scopus, and Embase). A filter was applied in all databases to include studies on human adults aged 18 and above. There was no language restriction. A systematic review on records from the inception of each database to November 2014 was published here. Content on this website will be updated once a year using the same search strategies as the original publication (see details here and a brief summary below).

Inclusion criteria Exclusion criteria
  1. Being related to sleep, operationalized as containing “sleep” in the title, abstract, or subject heading;
  2. An implied presence of daily sleep measures, operationalized as containing any of following terms in the title or abstract1: “actigraph*”, “diary”, “diaries”, “log(s)”, “daily”, “everyday”, “night*”, “day*”, “week*”, “month*”;
  3. An implied examination of IIV, operationalized as containing any of the following terms in the title or abstract: “variability”, “variation” “(in)stability”, “fluctuation”, “(ir)regularity”, “(ir)regular sleep*”, “(ir)regular bedtime”, “(ir)regular rise*”.
  1. Did not include adult humans aged 18 and above;
  2. Was not an empirical study;
  3. Was not a peer-reviewed publications;
  4. Sleep was not assessed daily, or daily assessment was for < 3 days, or sleep measurements were based on polysomnography;
  5. Daily IIV of sleep parameters were not quantitatively examined in relation to non-sleep related factors.

All title/abstract and full-text screening, and a randomly selected 25% of data extraction were carried out by 2 independent raters for reliability and accuracy. Data extraction form was based on the STROBE statement.
